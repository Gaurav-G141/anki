# Physics GRE Prep & Grad-Application Pain Points

_Research compiled from web searches against physicsgre.com (Physics GRE Discussion Forums), Physics Forums, The GradCafe, Reddit, and adjacent sources. physicsgre.com itself is behind Cloudflare (403 on direct fetch), so findings below are drawn from search-result titles and snippets, not full thread reads. Each item is tagged **[stated]** when the pain point appears in the result snippet text, or **[inferred]** when drawn only from the thread title._

## Summary

Across the Physics GRE community, the dominant frustrations cluster into a handful of recurring themes:

1. **Running out of practice material.** There are only ~6-10 real released ETS tests; serious students exhaust them, then plateau, then have nothing fresh to practice on. This is the single most app-relevant pain point.
2. **Time pressure / speed.** ~100 questions in 170 minutes (~1.7 min/question). Students repeatedly report not finishing — stopping at question 40, 59, or running out with 15 minutes left — and losing 100+ points purely to pacing rather than knowledge.
3. **Memorization burden.** No formula sheet is provided (only a table of constants). Students who relied on formula sheets in coursework are "out of practice" remembering equations and constants under pressure.
4. **Weak/forgotten topics.** Quantum mechanics and E&M are the most-cited hard areas; atomic physics, thermo/stat-mech, special relativity, and lab methods are commonly forgotten or under-covered in coursework. Many take the test before finishing the relevant courses.
5. **Score plateaus.** Students grind and stall; advice centers on targeting weak areas, redoing missed problems without peeking at solutions, and timed retakes — exactly the workflow a spaced-repetition app supports.
6. **Anxiety, burnout, and motivation collapse.** The forum is described as "a helpful stress reliever at times and a massive stress inducer at others." Rejection, low scores, and the waiting game drive discouragement, loss of interest, and burnout.
7. **Resource quality complaints.** The standard prep book ("Conquering the Physics GRE") is heavily relied on but criticized for typos/errors across editions; Griffiths textbooks are seen as poor for self-study; textbook problems are "way more complex than the GRE" and don't prepare you well.
8. **Uncertainty about whether to study / take it at all.** The test is now optional or dropped at many programs, so students agonize over whether prep time is even worth it — and whether a score helps or hurts.

I identified **30+ distinct forum/community sources** (see citations throughout; counted at the end).

## Theme 1 — Running out of practice material / limited official content

The most directly app-relevant frustration.

- **[stated]** Students exhaust everything available: one read "Conquering" cover to cover, did all end-of-chapter problems, all in-book practice exams, plus released 2001-2008 and 2017 tests, then plateaued and resorted to redoing old tests from memory. — "How to practice for physics gre when out of study material?" https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=173830
- **[stated]** Advice when materials run out: take a ~1-month break to "clear your memory" of practice problems so old tests feel fresh again — i.e., the community is manually reinventing spaced repetition. — same thread, https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=173830
- **[stated]** Recycled-question concern: standardized tests "pull from question databanks with only slight variations year to year," so going through old tests directly boosts your odds — but supply is finite. — "Low Score / plateau" discussion, https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=532
- **[inferred]** Demand for more problems: "Physics GRE Practice Problems," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=1073 ; "Working Practice Problems on the Physics GRE," http://www.physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=1063 ; "Practice problems in EM and optics," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=2600
- **[stated]** Fallback when official material is gone: "get copies of US textbooks and go through their problems chapter by chapter" and work "thousands upon thousands" of problems — a heavy, unstructured workaround. — https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=173830

## Theme 2 — Time pressure and slow problem-solving

- **[stated]** A student spent their entire 2-month vacation and still couldn't finish a single practice test; self-described as "very slow." — "Physics GRE first time practicing — Need advice for improvement," https://www.physicsforums.com/threads/physics-gre-first-time-practicing-need-advice-for-improvement.1065199/
- **[stated]** Classic pacing failure: reached question 40 with only 15 minutes left after devoting all free time to studying for three months — called it "the epitome of fail." — "Feeling overwhelmed and discouraged- looking for suggestions," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=5723
- **[stated]** Regret framed as a speed problem: "only finished 59 questions and got a 660; could have done much better if able to do computations faster." — "If you could go back….," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=679
- **[stated]** Top scorers warn the bottleneck is speed, not knowledge: people who practice only at PGRE difficulty "fail to finish the test in 2h50m"; you must practice harder/faster problems (e.g., Irodov) to build time-saving tricks. — "Please, help from people who scored higher than 900 in PGRE," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=3965
- **[stated]** Speed is the differentiator: "what determines better performance… is ability to solve problems quickly," achieved through repeated timed practice. — "Low Score on the Physics GRE," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=532
- **[inferred]** "Re-Take Physics Gre? How to improve guessing?" https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=5721

## Theme 3 — Memorization burden (no formula sheet)

- **[stated]** No formula sheet is allowed — only a table of constants. Students must build a personal formula sheet of equations they understand and can recall under pressure. — "Memorizing Formulas," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=3957
- **[stated]** Students are "out of practice remembering [formulae]" because coursework let them use formula sheets — direct gap between how they learned and how they're tested. — "How much of the material do you need to memorize?" https://www.physicsforums.com/threads/how-much-of-the-material-formulas-etc-do-you-need-to-memorize.723809/
- **[stated]** Uncertainty about which constants to memorize (proton mass, Boltzmann, Planck, electron charge, eV-to-J); advice to memorize trig values and useful combos like hc = 12.4 keV·Å, (kT)_room = 1/40 eV. — "memorizing constants," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?f=18&t=3981
- **[inferred]** "What things are you planning to memorize," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=1726 ; "Basic Physics Equations and Formulas," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=1065
- **[stated]** Students literally resort to memory-palace techniques to learn all the formulas — signal that current tools don't support retention. — Art of Memory forum, https://forum.artofmemory.com/t/constructing-my-first-memory-palace-learning-all-the-physics-formulas-for-the-physics-gre/28524
- **[stated]** Flashcard friction is real: Anki "requires manual card creation and has a steep learning curve," and generic decks "don't match your specific course." — https://flashrecall.app/blog/anki-physics

## Theme 4 — Weak / forgotten topics (Quantum, E&M, and more)

- **[stated]** Quantum and E&M flagged as the hardest: mark every QM/E&M miss on practice tests and drill them again. — "Best Quantum Mechanics text," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=73 ; "The key to success on Physics GRE…," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=1782
- **[stated]** Many sit the test before finishing E&M or QM coursework and must self-study; "90% of E&M and quantum questions should have been covered in lower-level coursework" but the gap still stresses people. — "How to prepare for PGRE if I haven't taken E&M or Quantum yet?" https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=179021
- **[stated]** Structural gap: some colleges offer only one semester of each core subject (E&M, QM, stats, mech), leaving students under-prepared. — "What to do: College only offers one semester in core Physics classes," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=182021
- **[stated/context]** Topic weights show the breadth to cover: Mechanics 20%, E&M 18%, QM 12%, Atomic 10%, Thermo/Stat 10%, Optics/Waves 9%, Special Relativity 6%, Lab Methods 6%, Specialized 9%. Special relativity and fluid mechanics are commonly "forgotten" or not covered. — PrepScholar / OSU / Kaplan summaries, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/physics-gre-practice-scores/ , https://physics.osu.edu/physics_gre
- **[stated]** Transition/non-traditional students (ECE, chemical engineering) face the largest topic gaps. — "Transitioning from ECE to Physics," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=181855 ; "chemical engineering B.S. to physics Ph.D.," http://www.physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?f=29&t=4914

## Theme 5 — Score plateaus and "how do I actually improve?"

- **[stated]** Canonical improvement workflow: after grading a practice test, do NOT look at the solution manual first — mark wrong answers, re-attempt them yourself, then review. Track weakest subjects and target them (don't re-study what you're already good at). — "Low Score on the Physics GRE," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=532
- **[stated]** Plateau-breaking advice: a 1-month break + timed retakes; improvements are "definitely possible, especially if your issues are only time-management." — https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=173830
- **[stated]** Big swings are possible and motivating: reports of 840→900→980 across three attempts, and "+300 points" from intensive timed-test practice — but only via huge volume. — https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=532 ; https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=179021
- **[inferred]** "Bad Physics GRE Score," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=6174 ; "Please give any advice on my overall scores…," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?f=28&t=5270
- **[stated]** Insight that reframes the whole task: "It's not about knowing the subjects, it's about knowing the GRE" — i.e., pattern familiarity and recall, the sweet spot for spaced repetition. — https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=179021

## Theme 6 — Anxiety, burnout, motivation, and the emotional toll

- **[stated]** The forum self-describes as "a helpful stress reliever at times and a massive stress inducer at others." — physicsgre.com discussion, https://physicsgre.com/
- **[stated]** Meta-anxiety about the community itself fueling competition: average scores "increasing every year." — "Is there fear that this website isn't in our best interest?" https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=1380
- **[inferred/stated]** Discouragement and loss of motivation: "Feeling overwhelmed and discouraged- looking for suggestions," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=5723 ; "Loss of interest in physics," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=3743
- **[stated]** Failure framing and recovery questions: "I just failed the Physics GRE, now what?" https://www.physicsforums.com/threads/i-just-failed-the-physics-gre-now-what.450018/
- **[stated]** General physics-student burnout is rampant (zero drive by 3rd year, crashing "once or twice a year") — the PGRE lands on top of an already-exhausted population. — https://www.physicsforums.com/threads/how-do-you-deal-with-physics-burnout.694097/
- **[inferred]** Career-level regret: "anyone regret choosing physics as their careers?" https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=4387 ; "What I wish I had been told as an undergrad," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=892

## Theme 7 — Complaints about existing resources / solutions

- **[stated]** "Conquering the Physics GRE" (the de facto standard book) has "numerous errors and typos," with official errata across editions 1, 2, and 3 — students can't fully trust the answer keys. — "Conquering the Physics GRE - Amazon reviews," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=6300 ; errata PDFs at physicsgreprep.com
- **[stated]** Griffiths textbooks (QM, E&M) are "less suitable if you're learning on your own" — key concepts need an instructor to flag. — https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=73
- **[stated]** Textbook problems mislead: "9/10 problems in textbooks will be WAY more complex than anything on the GRE, and actually won't prepare you that well." — https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=179021
- **[inferred]** "Rant About Textbooks by Griffiths," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=523
- **[stated]** Solution-dependency anti-pattern: students lean on solutions manuals instead of working problems, which the community explicitly warns against. — https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=532

## Theme 8 — "Should I even take/study for it?" (test-optional uncertainty)

- **[stated]** Many programs now don't require (or even refuse) the PGRE; some "tell applicants outright not to send them." — "Should I give the Physics GRE in 2023?" https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=182210
- **[stated]** Astronomy programs increasingly dropping the requirement. — "Some Astronomy programs dropping Physics GRE requirement," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=6468
- **[stated]** Research finding amplified in forums: a low score "could actually harm" admission chances; GPA predicts better than PGRE. — MSU research, https://natsci.msu.edu/news/msu-research-supports-lifting-the-weight-of-physics-gre-in-admissions.aspx
- **[stated/context]** Requirements are inconsistent and pages go stale, forcing per-program verification — decision paralysis over whether prep time pays off. — greguide.com database, https://greguide.com/gre-subject-tests/

## Theme 9 — Grad-application anxieties beyond the test itself

(The forum's largest section is applications, not the test — broader context for an app's value prop.)

- **[stated]** Rejected-everywhere despair and reapplication churn (rewriting SOPs, reshuffling rec letters). — "Unexpectedly rejected almost everywhere, Please explain..," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=5081 ; "Got rejected from almost everywhere," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=6329
- **[stated]** International-student pressure: needing 800-900+ to be competitive, higher cutoffs, and harder/more-expensive funding. — "Special Concerns for International Students," https://physicsgre.com/viewforum.php?f=28 ; "My Advice to International Students," https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=181859
- **[inferred]** Annual profile threads where applicants compare scores and outcomes (anxiety-driving social comparison): 2022-2026 "Applicant Profile and Admission Results" threads, e.g. https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=182366 (2026), https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=182347 (2025)
- **[inferred]** "Am I competitive enough for my school list?" https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=178971 ; "what should I do," http://www.physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?f=3&t=875

## Design implications (pain point → what a study app could solve)

| # | Pain point                                                                                                              | App opportunity                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| - | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1 | **Only ~6-10 real tests exist; serious students exhaust them and plateau**                                              | Large, fresh, GRE-calibrated question bank so students never "run out." Generate/curate problems that match real PGRE difficulty (not textbook difficulty). Track which items each user has seen and resurface them on a spacing schedule instead of forcing a manual "1-month memory break."                                  |
| 2 | **Pacing failures lose 100+ points despite knowing the material**                                                       | Built-in timed mode at the real ratio (~1.7 min/question), per-question timers, and analytics that separate "got it wrong" from "ran out of time." Drill "fast first-pass" behavior; flag slow questions for spaced review. Teach dimensional-analysis / limit-check / order-of-magnitude shortcuts as first-class card types. |
| 3 | **No formula sheet; students out of practice recalling equations/constants**                                            | Spaced-repetition flashcards for equations, constants, and useful combinations (hc=12.4 keV·Å, kT_room=1/40 eV, trig values). Auto-build a personalized formula deck from the questions a user misses. Lower the card-creation friction that drives people away from raw Anki.                                                 |
| 4 | **QM/E&M (and forgotten relativity, atomic, thermo, lab methods) are weak spots; many test before finishing courses**   | Topic-tagged content with the real PGRE weightings; surface a per-topic mastery dashboard. Auto-detect weak areas and bias the spaced-repetition queue toward them (the community's manual "target your weakest subject" advice, automated). Self-study tracks for students who haven't taken E&M/QM yet.                      |
| 5 | **Plateaus; the proven workflow (re-attempt misses without peeking, retest timed) is manual and tedious**               | Enforce the good workflow automatically: hide solutions on first attempt, queue missed problems for re-attempt later, then re-test under time. Show longitudinal score trajectory to make the 840→900→980 type progress visible and motivating.                                                                                |
| 6 | **Anxiety, burnout, social-comparison stress, motivation collapse**                                                     | Low-stress, steady daily review (spaced repetition's core selling point vs. cramming) instead of all-or-nothing practice tests. Progress/streak feedback and realistic score predictions to reduce uncertainty. Avoid leaderboard-style comparison that the forum itself worries about.                                        |
| 7 | **Standard prep book has trusted-but-buggy answer keys; textbooks mislead on difficulty; Griffiths bad for self-study** | Verified, explained solutions (the differentiator) calibrated to actual GRE difficulty. Worked, step-by-step explanations that don't require an instructor — directly addressing the "Griffiths needs a professor" gap.                                                                                                        |
| 8 | **"Is it even worth studying for / will a score help or hurt?"**                                                        | An up-to-date, per-program requirement tracker (required / optional / not accepted) plus a realistic "what score helps you here" signal, so users can decide whether and how hard to study — reducing decision paralysis.                                                                                                      |
| 9 | **Broader application anxiety (school lists, international cutoffs, reapplication)**                                    | Optional companion features: school-list builder with score/funding context, especially the international-student funding/cutoff realities, to extend value past test day.                                                                                                                                                     |
