## Problem 1

**Topic:** Electromagnetism ŌåÆ RC Circuit
**Answer:** B

An RC circuit has a switch that initially connects to point _a_ (charging) and is then thrown to point _b_ (discharging). The question asks for the plot of current vs. time during discharge.

**Solution:** During charging, $Q/C = V(1-e^{-t/RC})$with final charge $Q_0 = CV$. During discharge, $Q(t) = Q_0 e^{-t/RC}$, so the current is $I(t) = -dQ/dt = (V/R)e^{-t/RC}$, with initial value $I(0) = V/R$. Plots A, C, and E are eliminated because they do not show exponential decay. Choice B correctly shows exponential decay starting from $V/R$.

## Problem 2

**Answer:** B

A circuit contains a 5.0 V battery and a 10 ╬® resistor. A 0.1 m ├Ś 0.1 m loop is in a magnetic field changing at 150 T/s. Find the current.

## Problem 3

**Topic:** Electromagnetism ŌåÆ Potential
**Answer:** B

Find the electric potential at a point on the axis, a distance $x$from the center of a charged ring (radius $R$, total charge $Q$).

**Solution:** Using $V = \int \frac{dq}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r}$with $r = \sqrt{R^2 + x^2}$(constant for all ring elements):
$$V = \frac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0 \sqrt{R^2 + x^2}}$$At $x = 0$this reduces to the potential at distance $R$, confirming the form.

## Problem 4

**Topic:** Mechanics/E&M ŌåÆ Small Oscillations
**Answer:** A

A charge $q$of mass $m$is placed on the axis of the charged ring (from problem 3). Find the angular frequency of small oscillations about the center.

**Solution:** Expand the potential for $R \gg x$: $V \approx \frac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0 R}\left(1 + \frac{x^2}{2R^2}\right)$. The force is $F = qE_x = -q\,\partial V/\partial x = -\frac{qQ}{4\pi\epsilon_0 R}\frac{x}{R^2} = m\ddot{x}$. This is SHM with $\ddot{x} = -\omega^2 x$, giving:
$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{qQ}{4\pi\epsilon_0 R^3 m}}$$

## Problem 5

**Topic:** Mechanics ŌåÆ Centripetal Force
**Answer:** B

A car travels in a circular path while experiencing air resistance. Find the net horizontal force of the road on the car's tires (vector diagram).

**Solution:** Horizontal (along motion): $\sum F_x = 0 = -F_{air} + f_x$, so the road provides a forward force balancing air drag. Radial: $\sum F_y = -mv^2/r = f_y$, an inward centripetal component. The resultant of these two perpendicular components is the net horizontal road force, given by vector choice (B).

## Problem 7

**Topic:** Mechanics ŌåÆ Elastic Collisions
**Answer:** A

A ball of mass $m$dropped from height $h$collides elastically with a stationary mass $2m$. To what height does the ball rebound?

**Solution:** Impact speed $v_0 = \sqrt{2gh}$. Momentum: $mv_0 = -mv_1 + 2mv_2$. Energy: $mv_0^2 = mv_1^2 + 2mv_2^2$. Solving: $v_1 = v_0/3$, $v_2 = 2v_0/3$. Rebound height: $\frac{1}{2}mv_1^2 = mgh' \Rightarrow h' = \frac{v_0^2}{18g} = \frac{2gh}{18g} = \frac{h}{9}$.
