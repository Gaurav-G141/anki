## Problem 1

**Topic:** Quantum Mechanics → Momentum Operator
**Answer:** C

The wave function of a particle is $e^{i(kx-\omega t)}$where $x$is distance, $t$is time, and $k$and $\omega$are positive real numbers. Find the x-component of the momentum.

Choices: (A) 0 (B) $\hbar\omega$(C) $\hbar k$(D) $\frac{\hbar\omega}{c}$(E) $\frac{\hbar k}{\omega}$

**Solution:** The momentum operator is $\hat{p}_x = \frac{\hbar}{i}\frac{\partial}{\partial x}$. Applying it:
$$p_x\psi = \frac{\hbar}{i}\frac{\partial}{\partial x}e^{i(kx-\omega t)} = \frac{\hbar}{i}(ik)e^{i(kx-\omega t)} = \hbar k\,\psi$$The eigenvalue (momentum) is $\hbar k$.

## Problem 2

**Answer:** D

The longest wavelength x-ray that can undergo Bragg diffraction in a crystal for a given family of planes of spacing $d$is:

Choices: (A) $d/4$(B) $d/2$(C) $d$(D) $2d$(E) $4d$

**Solution:** Bragg's law: $\lambda = 2d\sin\theta$. The wavelength is maximized when $\sin\theta = 1$(at $\theta = 90°$):
$$\lambda_{max} = 2d\sin(90°) = 2d$$

## Problem 3

**Topic:** Quantum Mechanics → Bohr Theory
**Answer:** A

Determine the ratio of characteristic K x-ray energies between carbon ($Z=6$) and magnesium ($Z=12$).

Choices: (A) 1/4 (B) 1/2 (C) 1 (D) 2 (E) 4

**Solution:** For hydrogen-like atoms, characteristic K x-ray energy $\propto Z^2$. Therefore:
$$\frac{E_C}{E_{Mg}} = \frac{Z_C^2}{Z_{Mg}^2} = \frac{6^2}{12^2} = \frac{36}{144} = \frac{1}{4}$$(Moseley's law with $(Z-1)^2$gives $\approx 25/121 \approx 1/5$, still closest to 1/4.)

The magnitude of the Earth's gravitational force on a point mass is $F(r)$, where $r$is the distance from the Earth's center. Assume the Earth is a homogeneous sphere of radius $R$. What is $F(R)/F(2R)$?

## Problem 5

**Topic:** Mechanics → Gauss's Law (Gravitational)
**Answer:** C

Same setup as Problem 4. A shaft allows placing the mass at radius $R/2$. Find $F(R)/F(R/2)$.

Choices: (A) 8 (B) 4 (C) 2 (D) 1/2 (E) 1/4

## Problem 6

**Topic:** Mechanics → Statics / Friction
**Answer:** D

Two wedges, each of mass $m$, are placed next to each other on a flat floor. A cube of mass $M$is balanced on the wedges (45°). There is no friction between cube and wedges, but a coefficient of static friction $\mu < 1$exists between wedges and floor. What is the largest $M$that can be balanced without the wedges moving?

Choices: (A) $m/\sqrt{2}$(B) $\mu m/\sqrt{2}$(C) $\mu m/(1-\mu)$(D) $2\mu m/(1-\mu)$(E) All $M$will balance

**Solution:** Each wedge supports half the cube's weight; normal force on the floor from one wedge is $N = (m + M/2)g$, giving max friction $\mu(m+M/2)g$. The horizontal force pushing a wedge outward is $Mg/2$. For no slipping:
$$\mu\left(m + \frac{M}{2}\right)g \ge \frac{Mg}{2}$$$$\mu m \ge \frac{M(1-\mu)}{2} \implies M \le \frac{2\mu m}{1-\mu}$$

## Problem 7

**Topic:** Mechanics → Normal Modes
**Answer:** B

A cylindrical tube of mass $M$slides on a horizontal wire. Two identical pendulums, each of mass $m$and length $l$, hang from the ends of the tube. The system undergoes small oscillations. Which is a normal mode frequency? (The missing one of three.)

Choices: (A) $\sqrt{\frac{g(M+2m)}{lM}}$(B) $\sqrt{g/l}$(C) $\sqrt{\frac{g(M+m)}{lM}}$(D) $\sqrt{\frac{gm}{lM}}$(E) $\sqrt{\frac{gm}{l(M+m)}}$(F) $\sqrt{\frac{gm}{l(M+2m)}}$

**Solution:** The system has three modes: a zero-frequency translation, a symmetric mode $\sqrt{g(M+2m)/(lM)}$, and an antisymmetric mode in which the two pendulums swing oppositely so the tube stays fixed, giving a frequency independent of $M$: $\sqrt{g/l}$. The only $M$-independent choice is (B).

## Problem 8

**Topic:** Mechanics → Torque (Cross Product)
**Answer:** C

A solid cone hangs from a frictionless pivot at origin O. Which force $\mathbf{F}$applied at point P on the rim produces torque with $\tau_z < 0$? ($a,b,c$positive constants.)

Choices: (A) $\mathbf{F}=a\hat{k}$, P$=(0,b,-c)$(B) $\mathbf{F}=-a\hat{k}$, P$=(0,-b,-c)$(C) $\mathbf{F}=a\hat{j}$, P$=(-b,0,-c)$(D) $\mathbf{F}=a\hat{j}$, P$=(b,0,-c)$(E) $\mathbf{F}=-a\hat{k}$, P$=(-b,0,-c)$

**Solution:** $\tau_z = xF_y - yF_x$. Options A, B, E have $\mathbf{F}\parallel\hat{k}$giving $\tau_z = 0$. For (C): $\tau_z = xF_y = (-b)(a) = -ab < 0$. For (D): $\tau_z = (b)(a) > 0$. Answer is (C).

## Problem 9

**Topic:** Electromagnetism → Ampère's Law (Coaxial Cable)
**Answer:** A

A coaxial cable carries equal and opposite currents $i$on the inner and outer conductors. Find the magnetic field magnitude at point P outside the cable at distance $r$from the axis.

Choices: (A) Zero (B) $\frac{\mu_0 i r}{2\pi a^2}$(C) $\frac{\mu_0 i}{2\pi r}$(D) $\frac{\mu_0 i}{2\pi r}\frac{c^2-r^2}{c^2-b^2}$(E) $\frac{\mu_0 i}{2\pi r}\frac{r^2-b^2}{c^2-b^2}$

**Solution:** By Ampère's law, $\oint \vec{B}\cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_{enc}$. Outside, the enclosed current is $+i - i = 0$, so $B = 0$. This is the shielding property of coaxial cables.

## Problem 10

**Topic:** Electromagnetism → Image Charges
**Answer:** E

Charges $q$and $2q$are on the x-axis at $x=0.5a$and $1.5a$. An infinite grounded conducting plane is at $x=0$. Find the magnitude of the net force on charge $q$.

Choices: (A) $\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{q^2}{a^2}$(B) $\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{3q^2}{2a^2}$(C) $\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{2q^2}{a^2}$(D) $\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{3q^2}{a^2}$(E) $\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{7q^2}{2a^2}$

**Solution:** The grounded plane induces image charges $-q$(at $-0.5a$) and $-2q$(at $-1.5a$). Distances from $q$: to $+2q$is $a$, to image $-q$is $a$, to image $-2q$is $2a$. All forces on $q$point in the same direction:
$$F = \frac{q^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\left(\frac{2}{a^2} + \frac{1}{a^2} + \frac{2}{(2a)^2}\right) = \frac{q^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 a^2}\left(2+1+\tfrac12\right) = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{7q^2}{2a^2}$$

## Problem 11

**Topic:** Electromagnetism → RC Circuit
**Answer:** E

A charged capacitor is discharged through a resistor. How much time elapses until half of the capacitor's initial stored energy is dissipated?

Choices: (A) $RC$(B) $2RC$(C) $RC/2$(D) $RC/4$(E) $RC\ln(2)/2$

## Problem 12

**Topic:** Electromagnetism → Laplace's Equation
**Answer:** B

Two large conducting plates form a wedge of angle $\alpha$. One is at potential $V_0$, the other grounded. Find the potential as a function of angle $\varphi$(independent of $z$and $\rho$).

Choices: (A) $V_0/\alpha$(B) $V_0\varphi/\alpha$(C) $V_0\alpha/\varphi$(D) $V_0\varphi^2/\alpha$(E) $V_0\alpha/\varphi^2$

## Problem 14

**Topic:** Statistical Mechanics → Blackbody Radiation
**Answer:** C

A blackbody's radiation heats water from 20.0°C to 20.5°C in one minute. If the blackbody's absolute temperature is doubled and the experiment repeated, what is the final water temperature?

Choices: (A) 21°C (B) 24°C (C) 28°C (D) 36°C (E) Water would boil within one minute

**Solution:** By Stefan-Boltzmann, power $\propto T^4$. Doubling $T$gives $2^4 = 16\times$the energy. The original temperature rise was 0.5°C, so the new rise is $16 \times 0.5 = 8$°C. Final: $20 + 8 = 28$°C.

## Problem 21

**Topic:** Optics → Thin Film Interference
**Answer:** E

A vertical soap film ($n > 1$) viewed in reflected white light shows colored bands below but black at top. Which statements correctly explain this?
(I) top absorbs all light; (II) top thickness $\ll$wavelength; (III) 180° phase change for all wavelengths at front surface; (IV) no phase change at back surface.

Choices: (A) I only (B) II and III (C) III and IV (D) I, II, III (E) II, III, IV

**Solution:** At the thin top, the path difference is negligible; only the 180° phase shift at the front surface (reflection from higher index) remains, while the back surface (soap to air) has no shift.
